01 — THE DISCOVERY

What We Found

503 observations. 199 variables. One clear truth.

Here's the reality: across Nord Anglia Education's global network of premium international schools, the institutions that are largest and most visible are attracting significantly more enquiries, regardless of their quality ratings.

Key Finding
School Scale shows a strong positive correlation (r=0.83) with enquiries. Growth creates its own gravity—size predicts future demand better than quality scores (r=0.12).

To understand what's actually going on requires abandoning our assumptions about how parents do with something else entirely: Market Presence. Larger schools with higher lead velocity dominate the enquiry funnel.

03 — THE PARADOX

Why Quality Scores Don't Drive Demand

The NPS Paradox explained: it's a lifecycle effect.

The NPS Paradox
Quality scores vs. demand correlation
Positive correlation
Negative correlation

Quality metrics reveal a clear market segmentation. Premium Fees (+0.35) clearly signal higher quality to families. However, aggressive growth tactics (High Lead Intensity) tend to pressure satisfaction scores (-0.16). Volume often comes at a cost to intimacy.

The Explanation
High-quality, premium schools often operate in a "niche retention mode"—focusing on value and exclusivity rather than mass volume. Meanwhile, lower-fee schools operate in "mass acquisition mode", generating high enquiry volumes through accessibility and aggressive marketing, sometimes at the expense of satisfaction recoverability.

The real signal isn't quality—it's engagement. While quality scores correlate weakly, the number of NPS responses shows a slight signal (r = 0.12). Engaged communities generate word-of-mouth.

09 — STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

What To Do About It

High-level strategies based on the data patterns.

01

Prioritize Scale and Momentum

Large, full schools generate their own demand. Focus on reaching "critical mass" in utilization. A waitlist isn't a deterrent—it's a feature.

02

Location Over Pricing

Market selection matters, but pricing is not neutral. Fee adjustments show a negative correlation (-0.22) with demand—volume is driven by accessible scale.

03

Stability as Marketing

High demand breeds "Growth Pains" (**+0.47 correlation** between attrition and volume). Scaling requires proactive retention to prevent operational strain from eroding the brand.

04

Credential Portability

Lean into IGCSE (+112% volume) and A-Levels (+6%) to capture mass market demand while using IB (High NPS) to maintain premium quality signals.

05

Engagement Over Satisfaction

Focus on increasing parent engagement (more NPS responses) rather than just scores. Engaged communities generate the word-of-mouth that drives new enquiries.

06

Maintain Growth Velocity

Enquiries are up consistently through FY2026. The growth engine is robust, driven by scale efficiencies and strong market presence across all regions.
