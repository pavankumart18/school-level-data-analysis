# The Story

## Chapter 1: The Mystery

Here's something that shouldn't happen.

When we analyzed 544 school-year observations across Nord Anglia Education's network of 92 premium international schools, we found something that defied every assumption about how parents choose schools.

The schools that parents rated highest for education quality‚Äîmeasured through Net Promoter Scores on teaching, curriculum, and principal leadership‚Äîwere attracting **fewer enquiries** than their lower-rated counterparts.

Not more. Fewer.

## Chapter 3: Solving the Paradox

So why do higher-quality schools attract fewer enquiries?

The answer lies in understanding the **school lifecycle**.

### Acquisition Mode vs. Retention Mode

**Young, growing schools** are in acquisition mode:
- Building facilities, hiring teachers, running marketing campaigns
- Parent communities are transient‚Äîexpat families cycling through 2-3 year assignments
- Generate tons of enquiries because they *need* to
- NPS scores reflect the chaos of rapid growth (lower)

**Mature schools** are in retention mode:
- Parent communities are stable
- Word-of-mouth has replaced marketing
- Sky-high NPS scores
- Don't need as many enquiries because they're not constantly refilling departing cohorts

> **"High NPS isn't a cause of low demand. They're both symptoms of the same thing: maturity."**

# Strategic Recommendations

## 1. Prioritize Scale and Momentum
Large, full schools generate their own demand. Focus on reaching "critical mass" in utilization. A waitlist isn't a deterrent‚Äîit's a feature.

## 2. Location Over Pricing
Market selection (urban, FDI-rich regions) matters far more than fee adjustments. No fee variable shows significant negative correlation with demand.

## 3. Stability as Marketing
Reducing teacher attrition isn't just an HR goal‚Äîit's a demand driver. Staff stability signals institutional health to prospective families.

## 4. Credential Portability
Lean into IGCSE (+37% enquiries vs. non-IGCSE) and IB (+33%) programs to attract mobile expat demographics.

## 5. Engagement Over Satisfaction
Focus on increasing parent engagement (more NPS responses) rather than just scores. Engaged communities generate word-of-mouth.

## 6. Fix the Attribution Gap
20% of leads have no source. Solve this before optimizing spend on known channels.
